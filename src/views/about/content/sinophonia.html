Sinophonia 華品

Definition

I arrived at the term sinophonia 華品 to describe the focus of this site in a way that limits the awkward conflation of ethnicity, language, nationality and political designation that results when a single term is shared across such distinct concepts — in this case, Chinese.

Sinophone 華語圈 is an existing term that denotes individuals and populations who speak, read or write any of the various languages and dialects that originate from China.

In this vein, sinophonia refers to written or spoken materials that are produced by and for members of sinophone communities.

Cantonese cinema, Sichuanese theatre, and Hokkien songs all meet this definition, as do adverts, articles, books, magazines, online discussions, propaganda slogans, radio and television in these or any other languages or dialects of Chinese.

Translation

I translate sinophonia into Chinese as 華品.

華 refers to concepts of Chinese civilisation 華夏, language 華語, people 華人 and the diaspora 華僑. 華 is often used interchangeably with 漢 — e.g. sinophone 華語圈 may be rendered as 漢語圈 — but is a more neutral term, inclusive of those who identify as Chinese but not as members of the Han 漢族 ethnic group.

品 as a noun suffix is associated with terms for creative works 作品 and artworks 藝品, or more generally with goods 物品, articles 用品 and products 產品 / 貨品. As a noun prefix it has connotations of quality 品質, style 品格, aesthetic taste 品味 and moral character 品性.

Combining these two components forms a new compound word 華品 that closely reflects the definition of sinophonia.
