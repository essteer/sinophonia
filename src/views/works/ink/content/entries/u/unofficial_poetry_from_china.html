Unofficial Poetry from China

Leiden University Libraries' Unofficial Poetry from China is a digital archive that houses underground poetry journals and other materials, and which "is unique in that it combines a wealth of original material with online, research-driven, English-language documentation and contextualisation, and with online catalogue records and accessibility of the actual material to visitors from all over the world". 1

The collection began in the early 1990s with the doctoral fieldwork of Professor of Chinese Language and Literature Maghiel van Crevel, who sought the help of Chinese poets and scholars to acquire what has grown to become an extensive range of poetry journals published from the late 1970s to the present. With the establishment of the online repository, these materials are now freely available in digital format.

Notable journals featured in the archive include Enlightenment啓蒙, Not-Not非非 and Today!今天, which have each played important roles in launching the reputations of prominent modern poets in recent decades.

In addition to these materials, the archive hosts texts and lectures that introduce the history of the collection, the journals it curates, and various poetry movements that have taken place across the PRC over the past century.
