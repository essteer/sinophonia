Gao Xingjian 高行健

Gao Xingjian 高行健1940 is a novelist, playwright, translator and director. Born in Ganzhou, Jiangxi, Gao emigrated to France in the late 1980s and in 1997 became a French citizen. In 2000, he became the first writer in Chinese to receive the Nobel Prize in Literature "for an œuvre of universal validity, bitter insights and linguistic ingenuity, which has opened new paths for the Chinese novel and drama".

Soul Mountain靈山 is a fictionalised account of Gao's wanderings in southern China after having fled Beijing in advance of looming political persecution, and in the wake of a false cancer diagnosis.

Atop that fraught foundation stands a narrative that is jazz-like in its shifts of mood and style and time: Gao cuts an arc of dislocated passages that move from the 1st to 2nd to 3rd-person, as his protagonist's fragmented memories intersect with a restless pursuit of something other, be it ways to live and see, people to know and love and revel with, or that eponymous peak.
