Ai Weiwei 艾未未

Ai Weiwei 艾未未1957 is a multidisciplinary artist, documentary maker and political dissident. His father was the poet Ai Qing 艾青1910-1996 and his wife is contemporary artist Lu Qing 路青1964.

Ai is among the most famous artists of the 21st century, having received worldwide adoration and critical acclaim for an extensive body of work that spans architectural design, sculpture, documentary and visual art.

He is equally well-known for his political activism, which is at times indistinguishable from his artistic output. His committment to free expression has been a long-running source of friction with political authority, and has earned him recognition as a dedicated human rights advocate.

A notable example of Ai's blend of these aspects of himself is his former blogging activity on Weibo 微博, in which he shared candid details of his personal life alongside unrestrained social observations and criticism of the government.

His online activity was interrupted and much of his writing purged in 2009, in response to his efforts to document the devastation surrounding the 2008 Sichuan earthquake 汶川大地震2008·05·12.

Only fragments of the original blog survive in the public domain, but translations of a curated selection of his writing are in print as Ai Weiwei's Blog: Writings, Interviews, and Digital Rants, 2006-2009.
