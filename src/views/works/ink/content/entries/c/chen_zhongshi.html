Chen Zhongshi 陳忠實

Chen Zhongshi 陳忠實1942-2016 was an acclaimed author and the 1997 recipient of the Mao Dun Literature Prize茅盾文學獎.

The novel that solidified his reputation was White Deer Plain白鹿原, which follows two rival families in rural Shaanxi province through the major upheavals of the first half of the 20th century.

From the decline of the Qing dynasty 清朝1644-1912 through the establishment of the Republic of China 中華民國1912-1949, Japanese occupation and civil war, and the ultimate victory of the Communist Party, Chen depicts how conflicting interests and obligations lead individuals to fateful decisions.

Chen's narrative complexity brings out the overwhelming uncertainty people endured during the period, and avoids any sense of there being a reliable path to safety. His realist approach serves up vivid characters whose relative virtues or lack thereof offer little indication of where events will cast them. Of those who remain by the end of the novel, knowledge of what their real life equivalents would experience in subsequent decades adds weight to his central theme of individuals' insignificance in the face of existential struggle.
