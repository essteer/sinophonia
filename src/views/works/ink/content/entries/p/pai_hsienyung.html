Pai Hsien-yung 白先勇

Pai Hsien-yung 白先勇1937 is a novelist and short story writer. His father was the Kuomintang 國民黨 general Bai Chongxi 白崇禧1893—1966.

Pai's Taipei People 臺北人 is a collection of short stories about the eponymous city's residents, composed between the mid 1960s and early 1970s. Each narrative hints at or depicts outright the different forms of loss experienced by those who crossed the strait to Taiwan in the events around the Chinese Communist Party's 中國共產黨 victory over the KMT in 1949.

Be it loved ones, livelihoods or places, each of the protagonists is scarred in some way by what was left behind, even as they differ in their ability to adapt to their new reality.

Success in moving on seems to hinge on the insulation from the past that is afforded by youth or material wealth. Those less fortunate exist in denial with naive hopes to someday reclaim what was lost, or inhabit a state of detachment as they reflect on their former selves and how things used to be.
