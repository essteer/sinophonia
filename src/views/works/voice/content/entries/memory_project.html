The Memory Project 民間記憶計劃口述史

In 2010, documentary filmmaker Wu Wenguang 吳文光 launched The Memory Project to collect oral histories from survivors of the Great Famine 三年大饑荒 that swept the Chinese countryside between 1958 to 1961, resulting in the deaths of tens of millions of rural inhabitants.

The project has grown to involve the collaboration of over 150 filmmakers and more than 1,100 interviewees across hundreds of villages in 20 provinces, and thus presents an immense record of the countless personal tragedies that took place during the period, and which manifested to varying degrees according to differences in geography and policy. The subject focus has also since expanded to include other contemporaneous periods of national turmoil such as the Great Leap Forward 大躍進1958-1960 and the Cultural Revolution 文化大革命1966-1976.

The project's official website hosts a selection of featured interviews and related materials such as a timeline of key events and a map of interview locations. A larger body of the video interviews — numbering over 700 to date — is maintained at the digital collection of Duke University Libraries.
